Customer Experience
CODE
C
Care for Customers and Each Other
Make it personal • Lead with respect & safety
⌄

AT&T’s focus is always to serve customers first. When you Care for Customers and Each Other, you Make It Personal—show empathy, treat people with value and respect, and build trust.

Put yourself in the customer’s shoes. Recognize the difference between sympathy and empathy so you can see the situation from the customer’s point of view—especially when invited into a customer’s premises.

Key Ways to Personalize the Experience

  • Be a good listener, show empathy, and adjust your tone based on how the customer feels.
  • Respect customers and their individual differences.
  • Treat customers the way you want to be treated.
  • Ask thoughtful questions to understand the customer’s needs.
O
Own It
Own the complexity • Make it simple for customers
⌄

Owning It means being committed to finding the right solution for each customer—listen, anticipate needs, solve problems, and deliver outcomes that positively impact customers’ lives.

You Own It when you hold yourself accountable for delivering excellence and operating with integrity every day. Focus your message on the resolution to build customer confidence.

How to Take Ownership (Confidently & Effectively)

  • Listen to the customer’s needs first.
  • Ask insightful questions to confirm understanding.
  • Understand the issue and set realistic expectations.
  • Commit to finding the solution.
  • Keep it positive and focus on the resolution.
D
Deliver with Expertise
Be authentic • Do it right, document it right
⌄

Bring your own personality and professional integrity into the customer relationship. When you’re the expert, you can simplify the experience and communicate a clear plan of action.

Customers appreciate honesty and clear communication. Use what the customer tells you to develop a customer-centric solution.

Ways to Deliver with Expertise

  • Connect with the customer and bring your personality into the conversation.
  • Take ownership and commit to the solution.
  • Educate the customer on tools/services that enhance the experience (e.g., Smart Home Manager, extenders).
  • Manage expectations and clearly identify the plan.
  • Use available tools so you’re prepared for anything; offer solutions whenever possible.
E
Exceed Expectations
Make it matter • Create “wow” moments
⌄

Exceeding expectations means Making It Matter—putting the customer first and looking for opportunities to surprise and delight with exceptional service.

A “wow” moment can be a gesture, solution, or act of service that helps the customer feel valued and supported.

Ways to Exceed Expectations

  • Review the customer profile.
  • Use the customer’s name during interactions.
  • Sincerely thank the customer for being loyal to AT&T.
  • Ask about the customer’s experience to help manage expectations.
  • Go the extra mile to create “wow” moments.
CC
Crucial Conversations
De-escalate • Own it • Build trust
⌄

Handling crucial conversations well improves performance and builds confidence to Own It in any situation.

What’s True in Crucial Conversations

  • The customer’s perception is their reality.
  • Customers are not focused on internal AT&T day-to-day challenges (scheduling, inventory, system issues).
  • Active listening is essential to Owning It and handling the conversation.
  • You are empowered to improve the customer’s perception by Owning It and doing the right thing.

Listening Do’s

  • Give your undivided attention to the customer who is speaking.
  • Show that you understand with brief confirmations.
  • Listen for underlying needs in what the customer says.
  • Ask questions that encourage the customer to clarify their thoughts.
  • Summarize the main points from time to time.

Listening Don’ts

  • Don’t assume you know the issue or the customer’s experience.
  • Don’t interrupt the customer.
  • Don’t start telling your own stories or change the focus to yourself.
  • Don’t give advice, judge, divert the topic, or try to cheer the customer up instead of listening.
  • Don’t give your attention to anything/anyone other than the customer while they are sharing concerns.

Related Topics & Resources

Lost light

Confirm service state, inspect for damage, clean and scope connection points, then True Test toward the network before replacing parts.

Low optical power

Check connectors, jumpers, bends, contamination, and assigned path. Re-clean and re-test before escalating.

ONT activation issues

Verify assignment, activation sequence, registration status, light levels, and customer app readiness before closeout.

Wi‑Fi coverage issues

Run Wi‑Fi assessment, confirm gateway placement, identify dead zones, explain extenders, and review expected device performance.

B‑Test failures

Confirm path and configuration, repeat with clean connection points, verify uploads, and use escalation paths when results remain out of spec.

Customer education misses

Before leaving, confirm SHM setup, run a speed test, explain notifications, and answer questions in plain language.

Quick Start
User Guide / How to Use

What this app is for

This tool helps technicians stay aligned to the AT&T Expert Path during fiber installs and repairs. It is meant to keep work on track, reinforce accountability, and improve field quality.

Install vs Repair

Use Install for fiber installation jobs and Repair for service trouble / troubleshooting visits. The app automatically switches the markers and checklist based on the selected mode.

Expert Quest

Expert Quest is optional. It encourages extra quality behaviors such as photo uploads, customer Wi‑Fi education, and Smart Home Manager support.

Top quick-access tools

  • CODE: quick culture and quality expectations
  • Reference: field reminders and process guidance
  • Install References: install-specific checkpoints and notes
  • Related Topics & Resources: supporting topics and links
  • Share Feedback: submit app improvement ideas

Marker navigation

The horizontal marker rail lets technicians jump directly to a marker. Each section contains checklist items meant to keep the job aligned to the Expert Path and reduce missed steps.

Progress tracker

Sections done shows completed marker sections. Items done shows checked checklist items. Use this as a self-audit before closing the job.

Expand / Collapse / Reset

  • Expand all: opens all checklist sections
  • Collapse all: closes all sections
  • Reset progress: clears completed checklist items
  • Complete & Clear: quick review / clear action

Safety buttons

The lower safety buttons keep technicians mindful of critical pre-work reminders such as PPE, FVD, and job-site readiness before starting the main checklist.

Recommended workflow

  1. Select Install or Repair
  2. Review the safety reminders before starting work
  3. Use the marker rail or expand sections
  4. Check off items as work is completed
  5. Use references and resources when needed
  6. Review progress before closing the job

Feedback

Use Share Feedback to send suggestions, issues, or improvements. Submitting feedback will open your device’s email app with the information ready to send.

Safety Reminder — PPE & FVD (Foreign Voltage Detector)

PPE: Wear all required PPE for the job function (and any local/site requirements) before starting work.

FVD: Use a Foreign Voltage Detector when conditions warrant before contacting metallic enclosures/terminals. Treat circuits as energized until verified safe.

Why this matters

  • Foreign voltage can exist on customer wiring/plant and may not be obvious.
  • Verifying safety first reduces shock risk and prevents equipment damage.
  • Builds customer trust: safe, professional, consistent field behavior.

Before You Begin Work

  • Wear all required PPE appropriate to the job function.
    Examples: safety glasses, gloves, high-visibility vest; hard hat / fall protection where required.
  • Use a Foreign Voltage Detector (FVD) when conditions warrant before contacting metallic enclosures/terminals.
    Treat circuits as energized until verified safe. Verify tool function per local guidance.
  • Confirm job-site safety standards are met before starting work.
    Pause and reassess if conditions are unsafe (weather, pets, trip hazards, unsafe access).
